The Great State of Idaho

With a population of 1.939 million, Idaho stands as the 38th most populous state in the United States. Officially incorporated into the Union on July 3, 1890, Idaho has a rich history and cultural diversity. The state capital, Boise, is nestled in the treasure valley of southern Idaho. The majority of the state is comprised of more than 115 mountain ranges.

Idaho is considered to be one of the most rural states. Small pockets of civilization dotting a leviathan landscape of untamed natural wonder is the spirit of Idaho. Despite the isolation of the states population, agriculture, mining, and manufacturing thrive. Idaho may not be a powerhouse, however, it is self sufficient.

Syringa (Philadelphus lewisii): The State Flower of Idaho

Experience the mystique of the Gem State through the iconic Syringa (Philadelphus lewisii), the state flower. This shrub dots the mountains of Idaho with daziling white and green. It's foilage is important to maintaining a habitat for quail, deer, and elk.

    Syringa's most populated areas:

  1. Idaho mountain country
  2. Boise National Forest
  3. Anderson Ranch canyons

Embraced as a symbol of natural beauty, a pillar of ecological stability, and fragment of native history in the region. Local native tribes used it's branches to make arrows, pipes, and combs.
